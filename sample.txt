The global human population has reached approximately 8.3 billion people distributed across 195 recognized countries and 7 continents, with the vast majority residing in the Northern Hemisphere. India leads globally as the most populous nation with over 1.47 billion people, followed closely by China and the United States, while the average global life expectancy sits at 73.8 years. Geographically, Russia remains the largest nation by surface area, spanning 11% of the world's total landmass, while Africa's Congo River stands as the deepest river on Earth, dropping past 220 metres deep. Socioeconomic metrics, urbanization, and energy transitions—such as solar and wind installations overtaking fossil fuel growth—are tracked continuously by organizations like the World Bank.
